About me

Hi, I'm Evan! I'm an NDSEG Fellow and a Robotics Ph.D. Student at the Oregon State University Robotic Decision Making Laboratory where I'm advised by Dr. Geoff Hollinger. The goal of my research is to create safe motion planning and control algorithms for robots that operate in challenging environments, and to deploy these algorithms into the real world.

If I'm not in the lab working on my latest crazy idea, you can find me outdoors, training to hit a new PR in the gym, or playing video games with my friends.

Please feel free to contact me with any questions!

Problems that I'm thinking about

  • Motion Planning & Control

    How do we create motion planning algorithms for robots operating in the presence of nonlinear and time-varying dynamics?

  • Planning Under Uncertainty

    How do we account for uncertainty when designing planning algorithms for robots?

  • Safety

    How do we incorporate safety verification into robotic systems so that they operate in a safe, reliable way?

  • Field Robotics

    How do we overcome the challenges associated with deploying our systems into the real world?

  • pymavswarm Release

    August 13, 2022

    I released pymavswarm, a Python library used to safely control drone swarms and drone fleets with MAVLink. Some of the key features of pymavswarm are:

    • Easily send MAVLink commands to multiple drones, simultaneously
    • Multi-agent collision avoidance using reachability analysis
    • Monitor the state of swarm drone agents
    • Log incoming MAVLink messages for future evaluation and debugging

    The project can be downloaded from PyPI or from GitHub.
